Sea Otters

Sea otters are some of the most amazing creatures in the ocean. Unlike most marine mammals, they are not kept alive by layers of blubber - in fact, they have none. Instead, the sea otter's incredibly thick fur - there are more than 100,000 hairs per square centimeters - helps insulate the otter against the extreme cold of the water it spends the majority of it's life in (sea otters rarely, if ever, venture on to land).

A Member of Class Mammalia

Sea otters, like humans, are mammals, so they share all of the distinguishing features of mammals:

  • Milk production from mammary glands
  • Amniotic sac
  • Fur (hair), rather than scales or feathers
  • 4-chambered hearts
  • Endothermy
  • Don't lay eggs
  • Amnion
  • Bony tissue (Meaning that the bones are made of bone rather than of cartilige, as they are in some animals.)

Sea Otters As Marine Animals

Sea otters are well suited to their marine environment and possess many adaptations that allow them to function better.

The tail is used as a rudder to help navigate.
